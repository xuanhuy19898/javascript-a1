A Conversation with the Past
 An original short story by Xuan Huy Pham

 Nick, a second-year student at Mohawk College, had been eagerly anticipating the field trip to the Hamilton Museum of Steam & Technology. The idea of immersing himself in the history of Hamilton's industrial past filled him with excitement. As the bus rolled through the streets toward the museum, Nick couldn't help but feel a sense of anticipation building within him.

Upon arriving at the museum, Nick was immediately struck by its grandeur. The imposing brick facade spoke of a bygone era, a time when Hamilton was at the forefront of innovation and industry. Stepping through the doors, Nick was greeted by the sights and sounds of a bustling museum, filled with exhibits dedicated to the city's rich industrial heritage.

Hamilton Museum of Steam & Technology
Source: https://attractionsontario.ca/

Making his way through the museum, Nick found himself drawn to a particular exhibit - a collection of life-sized figures depicting the workers who had once toiled in Hamilton's factories and mills. As he examined the intricately detailed figures, Nick couldn't help but feel a sense of reverence for the men and women who had helped build the city he called home.

But as Nick stood before one of the figures - a burly steelworker frozen in time, he was struck by a strange sensation. It was as though the figure were staring back at him, its eyes alive with a spark of recognition. For a moment, Nick felt a shiver run down his spine, unsure of what to make of the experience.

And then, to his astonishment, the figure began to move. Its stiff limbs loosened, its rigid posture softened, and before Nick's eyes, it stepped down from its pedestal and approached him.

"Hello there," the figure said, its voice echoing with the weight of years gone by. "I must say, it's been quite some time since I've had the pleasure of conversing with a visitor."
